Overview of drills

Cordless drill/driver

Versatile for light tasks in wood, plastic and thin metal. Thanks to the battery you work cordlessly and flexibly. Some models include a hammer function for light masonry; useful for mounting and hanging.

  • Screwing and drilling in wood, plastic and plasterboard
  • Cordless working
  • Battery needs recharging
Hammer drill

Suitable when you need to drill through harder material such as brick. Combines rotation with a fast hammering action, ideal for securing something firmly into the wall.

  • Drilling in wood, metal and soft stone
  • Rotating motion + hammer function
  • Often corded (less flexible)
  • Not for concrete
Rotary hammer

For heavy-duty work. The powerful hammering action breaks through hard concrete and natural stone. Suitable for foundation work or making large holes (pipes).

  • Drilling in concrete and hard stone
  • Very high impact force
  • Not suitable for wood, metal or soft stone
Impact wrench

Tightens and loosens nuts and bolts quickly. Handy for larger constructions such as decking, garden houses or heavy furniture.

  • Loosening and tightening bolts and nuts
  • High torque
  • Risk of overtightening/damage
Drill press

For very precise drilling. Mounted on a stand, allowing you to make perfectly straight holes in wood, metal or plastic.

  • Highly accurate drilling (wood, metal, plastic)
  • Suitable for small/precision workpieces
  • Can only be used from the fixed stand
Demolition hammer

Heavy-duty demolition tool. Removes tiles and opens concrete floors. Not intended for drilling, but for breaking and chiseling.

  • Breaking and chiseling
  • Suitable for heavy demolition work
  • Not for drilling
