Working safely with saws

Saw types, PPE and extras at a glance

Overview of saw types, personal protective equipment and required additional tools.

1. Hand saw

Use

  • Manual sawing of wood, plastic and thin metal.
  • Useful for small precision jobs and work without power.
  • For example for battens, frame parts and plastic pipe.

PPE

Required

Not required

Dust mask

Extras

No extra tools are needed for this saw.

Watch out!

  • Check that the saw blade is sharp and undamaged.
  • A blunt blade increases the risk of slipping.
  • Always clamp the workpiece.
  • Never saw freehand.

2. Circular saw

Use

  • Straight cuts in sheet material and timber.
  • For floor panels, wall sheathing and roof decking.
  • Fast straight sawing in wood and sheet material.

Watch out!

  • Always use the blade guard.
  • Clamp the workpiece securely.
  • Do not cut freehand. Use a guide rail or fence.
  • Set the cutting depth to no more than 5 mm more than the material thickness.
  • Connect dust extraction wherever possible.

3. Jigsaw

Use

  • Curves, rounded shapes and cut-outs in sheet material.
  • For sockets, pipe penetrations and rounded shapes.
  • Suitable for wood, plastic and thin sheet metal.

Watch out!

  • Choose the correct blade for the material.
  • Clamp the workpiece securely. Vibrations can cause it to shift.
  • Always start a cut-out with a pre-drilled hole.

4. Reciprocating saw

Use

  • For demolition and dismantling.
  • Cuts through wood with nails, plastic pipe and light metal.
  • Useful in hard-to-reach places.

No extra tools are needed for this saw.

Watch out!

  • Hold the saw firmly with both hands.
  • Wear anti-vibration gloves during prolonged use.
  • Check in advance for hidden pipes or wiring.
  • Pay extra attention to kickback and vibrations.

5. Mitre saw

Use

  • Fast and accurate cross-cutting of battens, skirting boards and profiles.
  • Suitable for mitre cuts and compound cuts.
  • Widely used for interior work and finishing.

Watch out!

  • Keep hands outside the cutting zone.
  • Use the clamp to secure the workpiece.
  • Only remove the workpiece once the blade has come to a complete stop.
  • Connect dust extraction. This saw produces a lot of fine dust.

6. Table saw

Use

  • Rip cutting and straight cutting of large panels and long beams.
  • For sheet material, solid wood and composite panels.
  • With a rip fence for repeated accurate cuts.

Watch out!

  • Use a push stick for workpieces narrower than 15 cm.
  • Never keep your hands near the saw blade.
  • Adjust the riving knife correctly. Maximum 2 mm from the blade.
  • The riving knife must sit slightly lower than the highest tooth.
  • Use a guide rail with large panels.

7. Chainsaw

Use

  • For tree trunks, heavy beams and rough timber work.
  • On site for heavy timber and green maintenance.
  • This is the most dangerous saw type in this overview.

Watch out!

  • Check chain tension, brake function and chain oil.
  • Never cut above shoulder height.
  • Never work alone.
  • A chainsaw certificate is mandatory in many situations.
Close

Gloves

Protect against cuts, splinters and vibrations. For sawing, choose cut-resistant gloves, at least class 3. For chainsaw work: gloves with cut protection.

Close

Hearing protection

Mandatory for power saws that produce more than 80 dB(A). Use ear defenders or well-sealing earplugs. For prolonged sawing, hearing protection is essential to prevent permanent hearing damage.

Close

Safety glasses

Protects the eyes against flying particles, wood splinters and sawdust. Always wear safety glasses with side protection.

Close

Dust mask

Protects the airways against fine dust and wood dust. Use at least an FFP2 mask when sawing wood. FFP3 is recommended for hardwood, MDF or treated wood.

Close

Guide rail

A guide rail or fence that ensures a straight, controlled cut. Reduces the risk of wandering cuts and kickback.

Close

Dust extraction

A dust extraction system connected directly to the saw to capture fine dust at the source. It reduces exposure to wood dust and keeps the workplace clean.

Close

Chainsaw set

Consists of cut-protection trousers, a safety helmet with hearing protection and visor, and chainsaw boots or shoes with cut protection. This set is specifically required when working with a chainsaw.
