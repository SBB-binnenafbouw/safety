Ladders

Use a ladder or step ladder only when no safer option is possible.

  • Use only professional, inspected climbing equipment. No household step stools.
  • Make sure people can see that you are working at height.
  • Max 2 hours per day on a ladder
  • Keep items within arm's reach
  • Lift max 5 kg
  • Max wind force 6

Set the ladder at an angle of about 75 degrees

Maximum standing height is 5 meters

Ensure the ladder extends 1 meter above the platform

Keep 2 meters away from live electrical lines

Secure the ladder at the top and bottom

Keep 3 points of contact (two feet and one hand / two hands and one foot)

Step ladders

Use a ladder or step ladder only when no safer option is possible.

  • Use only professional, inspected climbing equipment. No household step stools.
  • Make sure people can see that you are working at height.

Do not use step ladders above 2.5 meters.

  • Max 2 hours per day on a step ladder
  • Keep items within arm's reach
  • Lift max 10 kg
  • Max wind force 6

Step ladder higher than 1 meter? Always use the support brace.

Mobile scaffolds

  • Only experienced people may assemble the mobile scaffold.
  • Make sure the mobile scaffold is inspected. Often indicated by a sticker, label, or colored marking.

Not sure how to check? Ask a supervisor on site.

Do not use fall protection on a mobile scaffold.

Climb from the inside. Use a floor hatch above 1 m.

Assembly:

  • Working platforms with toe boards
  • Intermediate platforms
    • 1st at 3.40 m height
    • then every 2.5 meters
  • Use four stabilizers and set the wheels on the brake
  • Use enough diagonals and bracing for a stable scaffold

Without anchoring:

  • Indoors: maximum 12 meters high
  • Outdoors: maximum 8 meters high
  • Never mix parts from different brands.
  • Do not use fall protection on a mobile scaffold.

Aerial lift

Check your surroundings and the ground before you start.

Do not drive near or over holes, openings, or other unstable areas.

Use the machine only on firm, level, and stable ground.

Do not work in strong wind or gusts (maximum wind force 6).

Do not work near live electrical lines.

Do not use the machine during lightning or storms.

Do not leave the aerial lift while you are at height.

Do not climb on the guardrails.

Do not place ladders, steps, or platforms in the basket.

While moving:

  • Move slowly.
  • Hold the guardrail securely. Watch your fingers.
  • Watch your head, and your feet if you are walking next to the lift.
  • Drive on public roads as little as possible.

Fall protection

Eliminating fall risk always comes first. Check whether barriers or edge protection are possible. Only if that is not possible do we use fall protection.

Required equipment:

  • Harness
  • Lanyard
  • Anchor point

Make sure your harness and connections can withstand at least 1000 kg.

Wear the harness properly adjusted: not too loose and not too tight.

Keep your harness clean and check for wear or damage.

Take as little equipment up as possible. Falling objects are dangerous.

Use an anchor point designed for fall protection. Do not attach to a railing or edge protection.

Make sure a relief step is attached to your harness so you can reduce the pressure on the straps after a fall.

Never work alone. If you fall, someone must rescue you within 20 minutes, because the leg arteries can be compressed.

Aerial lift: use fall protection specifically for aerial lifts (short lanyard). Attach to the anchor point, not the guardrail.

Floor openings

Small opening (< 1 m2)

  • Cover with load-bearing sheet material.
  • Fix in place at at least 2 corners.
  • Mark clearly, preferably with the text: "FALL HAZARD".

Large opening (> 1 m2)

Must be barricaded with guardrails, double handrail + toe board, or similar solutions.

Do not work without adequate protection of floor openings.

Scaffold

  • Never build a scaffold yourself. This is done by an experienced scaffolder.
  • Never modify a scaffold yourself. That is prohibited. Only a scaffolder may do this.

Keep the platform clean.

Do not take too much up, only what you need.

Make sure tools and materials cannot fall down.
